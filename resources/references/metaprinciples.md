# Metaprinciples: The Toolkit of Universal Truths

These are candidate "first principles" that apply across many domains. Use them as a checklist to find which ones constrain your problem.

## Physics & Engineering
| Principle | Statement | Application |
|---|---|---|
| Conservation of Energy | Energy cannot be created or destroyed, only transformed. | Every energy conversion has a max theoretical efficiency. |
| Conservation of Momentum | Momentum is conserved in a closed system. | Rocket equations, propulsion design. |
| Second Law of Thermodynamics | Entropy of an isolated system never decreases. | Why 100% efficiency is impossible; why cooling costs energy. |
| Speed of Light Limit | No information or matter exceeds c in vacuum. | Latency limits, communication constraints. |
| Square-Cube Law | Surface area grows with square, volume with cube of size. | Why large structures need different designs; heat dissipation. |
| Moore's Law (heuristic) | Transistor count doubles ~every 2 years. | Planning technology roadmaps. |

## Economics & Business
| Principle | Statement | Application |
|---|---|---|
| Supply & Demand | Price moves to balance quantity supplied and demanded. | Market viability of any product. |
| Marginal Cost Pricing | In competitive markets, price trends toward marginal cost. | Predicting long-term profit erosion. |
| Comparative Advantage | Trade benefits all parties when each specializes in their relative advantage. | Make vs. buy decisions. |
| Diminishing Marginal Utility | Each additional unit provides less additional value. | Feature prioritization, pricing tiers. |
| Network Effects | Value of a network grows non-linearly with its users (Metcalfe's Law). | Platform strategies. |

## Human Psychology & Sociology
| Principle | Statement | Application |
|---|---|---|
| Maslow's Hierarchy | Needs range from physiological to self-actualization. | Designing for different user segments. |
| Incentives Drive Behavior | People respond to incentives, even if poorly designed. | Organizational design, policy. |
| Dunbar's Number | Humans can maintain ~150 stable social relationships. | Community design, organizational scaling. |
| Loss Aversion | Losses feel ~2x as impactful as equivalent gains. | User experience, pricing communication. |

## Information & Logic
| Principle | Statement | Application |
|---|---|---|
| Gödel's Incompleteness | Any sufficiently rich formal system contains unprovable truths. | Limits of formal modeling. |
| Shannon's Information Theory | Information content relates to predictability. | Data compression, communication design. |
| Occam's Razor | Simpler explanations should be preferred if they fit the data equally well. | Hypothesis selection. |

## How to Use
1. During Phase 3, scan this list and identify which principles are immutable constraints on your problem.
2. Ask: "If I had to satisfy this principle perfectly, what would my solution have to look like?"
3. Some principles may not apply; that's fine. The goal is to find the few that do.
