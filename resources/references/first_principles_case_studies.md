# First Principles Case Studies

## 1. SpaceX Reusable Rockets
- **Prime Objective**: Place a payload into orbit at the lowest possible cost.
- **Key Destroyed Assumption**: "Rockets must be expendable."
- **Fundamental Elements**: Raw material cost of a rocket is ~2% of its price. The rest is manufacturing process and single-use design.
- **Reconstruction**: "If a rocket can launch, re-enter, and land with precision, it can be reused like an airplane."
- **Result**: Falcon 9 reduced launch costs by an order of magnitude.

## 2. iPhone Multi-Touch Interface
- **Prime Objective**: Maximize the area for information display while allowing intuitive control.
- **Key Destroyed Assumption**: "A mobile device needs a physical keyboard and stylus for precise input."
- **Fundamental Elements**: Humans have fingers as primary pointing tools; a screen can change dynamically.
- **Reconstruction**: "Replace fixed keys with a software keyboard that appears only when needed; use the entire surface as a display."
- **Result**: The modern smartphone interface standard.

## 3. Amazon Prime
- **Prime Objective**: Fulfill unchanging customer desires: lower prices, larger selection, faster delivery.
- **Key Destroyed Assumption**: "Shipping is a cost that must be passed to the customer per order."
- **Fundamental Elements**: Shipping cost is friction that reduces purchasing; membership can amortize it.
- **Reconstruction**: "Charge a flat annual fee for unlimited, fast shipping, removing the mental barrier entirely."
- **Result**: Prime became a key driver of Amazon's growth and loyalty.

## 4. Tesla Battery Cost
- **Prime Objective**: Energy storage at the lowest cost per kWh.
- **Key Destroyed Assumption**: "Battery packs cost $600/kWh and this is just the market price."
- **Fundamental Elements**: Battery = raw materials (lithium, cobalt, nickel, etc.) + assembly.
- **Reconstruction**: "If we buy materials at commodity prices and build the factory, what's the floor cost?"
- **Result**: Achieved costs below $100/kWh through vertical integration and scale.

## Patterns in All Cases
1. All start by ignoring the industry's answer to "what does this cost?" or "what form does this take?"
2. All decompose to basic physical elements, chemistry, or unchanging human needs.
3. All build back up from those fundamentals, unconstrained by precedent.
4. All produce solutions that, in hindsight, seem "obvious" because they align with fundamental truths.
